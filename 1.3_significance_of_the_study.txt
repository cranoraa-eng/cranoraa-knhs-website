1.3 Significance of the Study

PRISM was designed with specific users in mind, and its value becomes clearer when looking at what each group actually gets from the system.

Students. Through the student portal, students can view their grades across quarters, check attendance records, download report cards as PDFs, and access learning materials (DLPs, DLLs, modules, activity sheets) uploaded by their teachers. Assignments appear with due dates and submission status. The chat system supports private and group conversations with media sharing, message pinning, and reactions. Students can submit absence excuses, track their enrollment status, request records like transcripts and transfer certificates, and receive real-time notifications through WebSocket and Firebase push. They also have a scratch card option for looking up grades without logging in. In short, students get direct access to their own academic data without going through the registrar or waiting for a teacher to respond.

Teachers. For teachers, PRISM handles the repetitive work. Grade entry supports batch saving, and final grades are computed automatically using weighted components (Written Work, Performance Task, Quarterly Assessment) with configurable weights. The system applies the DepEd transmutation table to convert raw averages into the standard 1.00 to 5.00 scale. Attendance marking works on a schedule basis with bulk save, and parents receive push notifications when a child is marked absent or late. Teachers upload learning materials once and students access them anytime. The announcement system lets teachers target messages to specific audiences (all, students, teachers, parents) or individual classrooms. Class management includes tracking enrollments, generating grade reports with class rankings, and exporting data as CSV. Behavioral records and parent-teacher meetings are also handled within the system.

School Administrators. Administrators get a centralized view of the school through the analytics dashboard, which displays enrollment counts, grade distributions, attendance trends over 30 days, and subject-level performance using charts and summary tables. The enrollment pipeline handles everything from public application to document verification to approval, automatically creating student and parent accounts upon enrollment. Fee management tracks tuition, miscellaneous, books, and uniform payments with balance tracking. Every significant action is recorded in the audit log (18 action types including create, update, delete, login, grade changes, attendance marking, and exports). System settings cover site branding, grading defaults, maintenance mode, and feature toggles. Data can be exported as CSV for grades, attendance, and enrollment. The system also includes backup management, data retention policies, chat moderation with suspend and mute capabilities, and an emergency broadcast system for school-wide announcements.

Parents and Guardians. Parents get their own portal where they can monitor each linked child's attendance rate, recent attendance records, grades across subjects, general average, classroom adviser, and weekly schedule. They receive real-time push notifications (via FCM) when their child is marked absent or late. Year-over-year grade comparison shows quarterly averages grouped by school year. Report cards are downloadable as PDFs. Parents can view school announcements, send messages to teachers through the chat system, and create support tickets. Parent accounts are created automatically during the enrollment process, linked to the student through the ParentLink model.

Future Researchers. This study adds to the growing body of local literature on school information systems in Philippine public education. It documents a complete application of the ADDIE model in an educational software context, using Django REST Framework on the backend and React on the frontend, with real-time features through Django Channels and WebSocket. The system was evaluated by actual school users at Kiwalan National High School. Researchers in educational technology, information systems, or software engineering working on similar projects in comparable settings should find the methodology and evaluation data useful as a reference point.
